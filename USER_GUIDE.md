## Getting Started

### What is Thesis?

Thesis is a specialized AI assistant designed specifically for Learning & Development (L&D) professionals. Unlike general-purpose AI chatbots, Thesis is built on the **Bradbury Architecture Method** and focuses on creating **ROI-driven training programs** that deliver measurable business outcomes.

### Key Differences from ChatGPT/Other AI Tools

**Thesis is NOT just another chatbot.** Here's what makes Thesis different:

- **L&D Expert** - Trained on instructional design best practices, learning science, and behavior change
- **ROI-Focused** - Always ties learning solutions to business KPIs, not just course completion
- **Evidence-Based** - Grounded in Self-Determination Theory, Desirable Difficulties, and cognitive science
- **Context-Aware** - Upload your organization's documents and Thesis learns your context
- **Multi-Modal** - Generate text, images, curricula, assessments, and job aids
- **Production-Ready** - Every deliverable is client-ready with built-in measurement points

### Your First Conversation

When you start a conversation with Thesis, you can:

1. **Ask a question**: "How do I measure training ROI for a sales enablement program?"
2. **Request a deliverable**: "Create a facilitator guide for a 2-hour workshop on active listening"
3. **Analyze a problem**: "Our customer service scores are dropping. How can training help?"
4. **Generate visuals**: "Generate an image of a diverse team collaborating in a modern office"

Thesis will respond with expert guidance, ask clarifying questions when needed, and deliver professional-quality outputs.

## Core Concepts

### The DDLD Framework

Thesis uses the **DDLD Framework** to reverse-engineer training solutions from business outcomes:

1. **Data** - What business metrics are you trying to move?
2. **Desired State** - What does success look like in measurable terms?
3. **Learning Gap** - What knowledge/skill gaps prevent the desired state?
4. **Difference** - What's the minimal effective intervention to close the gap?

**Example Conversation:**
```
You: "We need sales training"

Thesis: "Let's start with the data. What business metric are you trying to improve?
For example:
- Revenue per rep
- Win rate
- Sales cycle length
- Customer acquisition cost

What specific number needs to move?"
```

### Thesis's Operating Modes

Thesis adapts his approach based on what you need:

- **Coach Mode** - Asks probing questions to help you think through problems
- **Developer Mode** - Creates deliverables (curricula, assessments, guides)
- **Analyst Mode** - Analyzes data, identifies gaps, recommends solutions
- **Advisor Mode** - Provides strategic L&D guidance and best practices

You don't need to explicitly switch modes - Thesis adapts automatically based on your request.

### Learning Science Foundation

Thesis's recommendations are grounded in research:

- **Self-Determination Theory** - Autonomy, competence, relatedness drive motivation
- **Desirable Difficulties** - Spacing, interleaving, retrieval practice improve retention
- **Transfer of Learning** - Focus on on-the-job application, not classroom knowledge
- **Behavioral Science** - Change behaviors through environmental design and feedback loops

## How to Talk to Thesis

### Natural Conversation

Thesis understands natural language. You don't need special commands or formatting for most tasks.

**Good Examples:**
```
"Help me design a leadership development program for mid-level managers"
"Create an assessment for a cybersecurity awareness training"
"What's wrong with using Kirkpatrick Level 1 smile sheets?"
"Generate a scenario where a manager gives difficult feedback"
```

**What NOT to Do:**
```
BAD: "Act as a L&D expert and..." (Thesis already is one)
BAD: Over-explaining common L&D concepts (Thesis knows them)
BAD: Asking for generic ChatGPT-style responses
```

### Being Specific

The more context you provide, the better Thesis's response:

**Less Helpful:**
```
"Create a training program"
```

**More Helpful:**
```
"Create a 3-week onboarding program for customer success managers at a B2B SaaS company.
Our main challenge is that new CSMs take 6 months to hit their quota. We want to reduce
that to 3 months. Focus on discovery calls and objection handling."
```

### Asking Follow-Up Questions

Thesis remembers your conversation history. Build on previous responses:

```
You: "Create a workshop outline for conflict resolution"
Thesis: [Provides outline]

You: "Make it more interactive with role-plays"
Thesis: [Updates the outline]

You: "Now create participant handouts for section 2"
Thesis: [Creates handouts]
```

- Interactive activities with facilitation notes
- Assessment strategies

- Feedback for each response option

**What You'll Get:**

### 5. Facilitator Support

**What Thesis Can Do:**

## Image Generation

Thesis can create training visuals in 3-5 seconds using AI image generation.

### What You Can Generate

- **Scenario Backgrounds** - Workplace settings (office, retail, healthcare, etc.)
- **Concept Visualizations** - Abstract ideas made concrete (leadership, teamwork, growth)
- **Diverse Characters** - Inclusive representation across age, ethnicity, ability
- **Job Aids** - Process diagrams, decision trees, checklists
- **Presentation Graphics** - Engaging visuals for slides and handouts

### How to Request Images

Just ask naturally. Thesis will automatically detect image requests:

**Examples:**
```
"Generate an image of a diverse team collaborating in a modern office"
"Show me a visual of a manager giving constructive feedback to an employee"
"Create an image of a customer service representative on a phone call"
"Generate a landscape image of a peaceful workspace for mindfulness training"
```

### What You'll See

1. **Your message appears** - Your request shows immediately
2. **Thesis responds** - Text explaining the image context
3. **Loading placeholder** - Animated "Generating image..." box appears
4. **Image appears** - Full image displays in 3-5 seconds

### Image Options

**Aspect Ratios:**
- **Square (1:1)** - "Generate a square image of..."
- **Landscape (16:9)** - "Generate a landscape image of..." (default)
- **Portrait (9:16)** - "Generate a portrait image of..."

**Style Guidance:**
```
"Generate an image of a training session in a modern classroom, professional
photograph style"

"Create a flat design illustration of a decision-making process"

"Generate a realistic photo of a healthcare team in scrubs discussing a patient case"
```

### Multiple Images in One Conversation

You can generate unlimited images in a single conversation:

```
You: "Generate an image of a team meeting"
Thesis: [Generates image]

You: "Now generate one showing conflict resolution"
Thesis: [Generates second image]

You: "One more - show a celebration after project completion"
Thesis: [Generates third image]
```

All images appear in chronological order with your conversation.

### Tips for Better Images

1. **Be specific about setting**: "in a modern office" vs "in a hospital break room"
2. **Describe diversity**: "diverse team of different ages and ethnicities"
3. **Specify mood**: "professional and collaborative" vs "tense and stressful"
4. **Mention key elements**: "showing a whiteboard with diagrams" or "people wearing hard hats"

## Document Upload & RAG

Thesis can learn from your organization's documents using RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation).

### What Documents to Upload

**Good Candidates:**
- Employee handbooks and policies
- Existing training materials and curricula
- Standard operating procedures (SOPs)
- Competency frameworks and job descriptions
- Past needs assessments and evaluation reports
- Industry-specific compliance requirements

**What NOT to Upload:**
- Personal information or PII
- Confidential business strategy
- Employee performance reviews
- Anything you wouldn't share with a consultant

### How Documents Enhance Thesis

Once uploaded, Thesis will:

1. **Reference your specific policies** when designing compliance training
2. **Use your terminology** instead of generic corporate speak
3. **Align to your competency models** when creating assessments
4. **Build on existing programs** rather than starting from scratch
5. **Maintain brand voice** and organizational culture

### How to Use It

**Upload Documents:**
1. Click the document upload icon
2. Select PDFs, Word docs, or PowerPoint files
3. Thesis processes and indexes them (usually takes 10-30 seconds)

**Reference in Conversation:**
```
"I've uploaded our sales competency framework. Design a training program that
develops the 'consultative selling' competency to mastery level."
```

Thesis will automatically pull relevant information from your documents.

### Google Drive & Notion Integration

Connect Thesis to your Google Drive or Notion workspace for seamless access:

**Benefits:**
- No manual uploads - Thesis can access authorized folders
- Auto-sync when documents change
- Maintain single source of truth
- Collaborate with team members on shared drives

**How to Connect:**
1. Go to Settings > Integrations
2. Authorize Google Drive or Notion
3. Select which folders Thesis can access
4. Thesis indexes content automatically

## Best Practices

### 1. Start with Business Outcomes

**Instead of:**
```
"Create a time management training"
```

**Try:**
```
"Our project managers are missing deadlines 40% of the time, costing us $200K in
client penalties. Design a solution to improve on-time delivery to 90%."
```

Thesis will help you determine if training is the right solution and design accordingly.

### 2. Iterate and Refine

Don't expect perfection on the first response. Build on Thesis's output:

```
You: "Create a leadership workshop outline"
Thesis: [Provides outline]

You: "Make it more interactive - less lecture, more activities"
Thesis: [Revises outline]

You: "Focus more on difficult conversations and less on strategy"
Thesis: [Refines focus]

You: "Perfect. Now create participant materials for module 2"
Thesis: [Creates materials]
```

### 3. Ask "Why" Questions

Thesis can explain the learning science behind recommendations:

```
"Why are you recommending spaced retrieval instead of a final quiz?"
"Why is this scenario better than a multiple-choice question?"
"Why focus on behavior change instead of knowledge transfer?"
```

Understanding the "why" makes you a better instructional designer.

### 4. Use Thesis as a Thought Partner

Don't just ask for deliverables. Use Thesis to think through challenges:

```
"I'm stuck on how to assess leadership skills without relying on self-reporting.
What are my options?"

"My stakeholder wants a 2-hour workshop but there's 8 hours of content. How do I
prioritize?"

"I'm getting pushback on removing content. How do I explain why less is more?"
```

### 5. Leverage Conversation History

Thesis remembers your entire conversation. Use this to build complex projects:

```
Session 1: Needs analysis
Session 2: Learning objectives
Session 3: Curriculum outline
Session 4: Assessment design
Session 5: Facilitator guide
Session 6: Participant materials
```

All in one conversation thread with full context.

## Troubleshooting

### Image Generation Issues

**Problem: Image isn't appearing**

- Check that you used clear image request language ("generate an image of...")
- Images take 3-5 seconds - look for the "Generating image..." placeholder
- Refresh the page if the image doesn't appear after 10 seconds

**Problem: Image doesn't match my request**

- Be more specific about setting, subjects, and style
- Try rephrasing your request with more detail
- Request another image: "Generate another version with more diversity"

**Problem: Wrong aspect ratio**

- Specify explicitly: "Generate a **portrait** image of..." or "Generate a **landscape** image of..."

### Conversation Issues

**Problem: Thesis seems generic**

- Provide more context about your organization, industry, and learners
- Upload documents so Thesis learns your terminology
- Be specific about constraints (time, budget, audience level)

**Problem: Response is too long/short**

- Ask Thesis to adjust: "Make that more concise" or "Expand on section 2"
- Set expectations upfront: "Give me a 1-page summary" or "Create a detailed 10-page guide"

**Problem: Missing important information**

- Thesis asks clarifying questions - answer them for better results
- You can always add details later: "Also include examples for each point"

### Document Upload Issues

**Problem: Document not processing**

- Ensure file is PDF, DOCX, or PPTX (other formats not supported)
- Check file size (maximum 10MB per file)
- Make sure document isn't password-protected

**Problem: Thesis isn't using uploaded documents**

- Explicitly mention them: "Reference the competency framework I uploaded"
- Check that upload completed successfully (you should see confirmation)
- Try re-uploading if the file was corrupted
