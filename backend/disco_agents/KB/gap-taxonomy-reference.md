# Gap Taxonomy Reference

A structured reference for the four gap types used in the Investigation Throughline. Referenced by the Discovery Guide agent when the throughline includes gaps.

## Gap Types

### Data Gaps
**Definition**: Missing, incomplete, or inaccessible information needed for decision-making.

**Typical Indicators**:
- "We don't have numbers on..."
- "Nobody tracks..."
- "The data exists but we can't access it"
- Decisions being made on assumptions rather than evidence

**Investigation Approach**:
1. What data exists? Where is it stored?
2. Who owns the data? Can we get access?
3. What format is it in? How current is it?
4. What's missing entirely? Can we collect it?
5. What would it cost (time/effort) to fill this gap?

**Example Questions**:
- "How many hours per week does your team spend on [process]?"
- "Where do you track [metric]? Can we see the dashboard?"
- "What reporting exists today? What's missing?"

---

### People Gaps
**Definition**: Missing expertise, knowledge, relationships, or organizational capacity.

**Typical Indicators**:
- "We don't have anyone who knows..."
- "The person who handled this left"
- "We need executive sponsorship but don't have it"
- Key knowledge concentrated in one person

**Investigation Approach**:
1. Who has the knowledge we need? Are they accessible?
2. What expertise is missing from the team?
3. Is there organizational capacity for this change?
4. Who are the decision-makers? Are they aligned?
5. Is there a sponsor who can remove blockers?

**Example Questions**:
- "Who would you go to if you needed to understand [topic]?"
- "Does your team have capacity to take on something new right now?"
- "Who needs to approve this before it can move forward?"

---

### Process Gaps
**Definition**: Missing, broken, or inefficient workflows and procedures.

**Typical Indicators**:
- "We do it differently every time"
- "There's no documented process"
- "It takes way too long because of [handoff/approval]"
- Workarounds and shadow processes

**Investigation Approach**:
1. What process exists today? Is it documented?
2. Where does the process break down? What's the bottleneck?
3. Who maintains the process? When was it last updated?
4. How long does each step take? What's the end-to-end time?
5. What workarounds exist? Why were they created?

**Example Questions**:
- "Walk me through what happens when [trigger event] occurs"
- "Where does this process typically get stuck or delayed?"
- "If you could fix one step in this workflow, which would it be?"

---

### Capability Gaps
**Definition**: Missing tools, technology, skills, or organizational abilities.

**Typical Indicators**:
- "We don't have a tool for that"
- "Our current system can't do..."
- "We'd need to build something custom"
- Manual processes that should be automated

**Investigation Approach**:
1. What can we do today? What can't we?
2. What tools/platforms exist? Are they being used effectively?
3. What skills would the team need to develop?
4. Build vs. buy: is there a solution we can acquire?
5. What would it take to close this capability gap?

**Example Questions**:
- "What tools do you currently use for [task]? What's missing?"
- "If you had unlimited budget, what capability would you add first?"
- "Is this something we need to build, or does a solution exist?"

---

## Using Gap Types in Discovery

### In Throughline Framing
When creating gaps in the investigation throughline, specify the type:
```
- [g-1] data: No metrics on current processing time per account
- [g-2] people: No executive sponsor identified for cross-department initiative
- [g-3] process: No documented workflow for onboarding new AI tools
- [g-4] capability: No platform for automated compliance checking
```

### In Discovery Planning
The Discovery Guide uses gap types to design targeted sessions:
- **Data gaps** → Research sessions focused on data discovery and quantification
- **People gaps** → Stakeholder interviews focused on expertise mapping and alignment
- **Process gaps** → Process mapping workshops with current practitioners
- **Capability gaps** → Technical assessment sessions with solution architects

### In Coverage Assessment
Coverage mode tracks gap closure status:
- **Addressed**: Gap has been investigated and information obtained
- **Partially addressed**: Some progress but more investigation needed
- **Still open**: Gap remains uninvestigated
- **No longer relevant**: Circumstances changed, gap doesn't apply
