# Comprehensive Question Libraries for Software Project Discovery
*A context-triggered discovery framework for uncovering hidden requirements and risks*

---

## **1. SERVICE/CONSULTANCY BUSINESS CONTEXT**

**Trigger:** Client mentions hourly billing, client deliverables, or professional services.

### **Primary Discovery Questions**

**Fee Structures & Scope Management**
- How do you currently track billable vs. non-billable time?
- What triggers scope change discussions with clients?
- What percentage of projects typically exceed original estimates?
- How do you handle "scope creep" from clients?
- What's your process for defining deliverables acceptance criteria?

**Client Qualification & Management**
- What disqualifies a potential client from working with you?
- How do you assess client technical capability/readiness?
- What's your client onboarding process duration and cost?
- What types of clients generate the most support burden post-delivery?

**Professional Liability**
- What professional liability insurance coverage do you carry?
- Have you had any claims or near-misses in the past 3 years?
- What contractual limitations of liability do you use?
- How do you handle errors/omissions in deliverables?

**Contracts & Payment**
- What payment terms have caused collection problems?
- Do you require retainers or milestone payments?
- How do you handle late payments?
- What termination clauses protect you from non-paying clients?

### **Follow-up Pathways**

**If client mentions "fixed-price projects":**
- "How often do fixed-price projects become unprofitable?"
- "What's your process for requirements specification before quoting?"
- "How do you handle change requests on fixed-price engagements?"

**If client mentions "retainers":**
- "How do you track and justify retainer usage?"
- "What happens when retainer hours are exhausted mid-month?"
- "How do you handle unused retainer hours?"

### **Red Flags & Required Probing**
- "We work with whatever clients come our way." → Probe client screening process
- "We don't use contracts for small projects." → Probe liability exposure
- "Clients often ask for 'small additions' at no charge." → Probe scope control
- "We invoice at project completion." → Probe cash flow risk

## **2. FORMS & DATA COLLECTION CONTEXT**

**Trigger:** Project involves forms, surveys, applications, or data entry.

### **Primary Discovery Questions**

**Form Completion & Abandonment**
- What's your current form abandonment rate?
- At which fields do users typically abandon?
- What's the average time to complete your longest form?
- How many form fields are absolutely required vs. optional?

**Mobile Considerations**
- What percentage of form submissions come from mobile?
- Have you tested forms on various mobile devices/platforms?
- How do you handle file uploads on mobile?
- Do mobile users show different abandonment patterns?

**Multi-session & Progress Saving**
- Do users need to save and return later to complete forms?
- How long should partially completed forms persist?
- How do users retrieve saved progress?
- Should form data auto-save during completion?

**Data Quality & Validation**
- What data validation happens client-side vs. server-side?
- How do you handle "correct but invalid" data (e.g., nicknames vs legal names)?
- What's your process for correcting submitted data errors?
- How strict should real-time validation be?

**SPAM & Security**
- What SPAM volume do you currently experience?
- What CAPTCHA or alternative approaches have you used?
- How do you detect and block automated submissions?
- Do you need to comply with any specific accessibility requirements?

**Conditional Logic & Calculations**
- What's the most complex conditional branching in your forms?
- Do any fields require calculations based on other inputs?
- How should conditional sections affect overall progress tracking?
- Do calculations need to be shown in real-time?

### **Follow-up Pathways**

**If abandonment rate is high:**
- "Have you identified specific problematic fields or steps?"
- "What testing have you done with progressive disclosure?"
- "How important is completion rate vs. data quality?"

**If discussing validation:**
- "What's the cost of false positives vs false negatives in validation?"
- "How should the system handle borderline valid data?"
- "Who corrects invalid submissions and how?"

### **Red Flags & Required Probing**
- "We need to validate everything in real-time." → Probe user experience impact
- "Users can save forms forever." → Probe data retention policies
- "No SPAM protection needed." → Probe historical SPAM volume
- "All fields are required." → Probe completion rate impact

## **3. PHYSICAL GOODS CONTEXT**

**Trigger:** Project involves e-commerce, inventory, or product fulfillment.

### **Primary Discovery Questions**

**Seasonality & Inventory**
- What's your peak vs. average inventory turnover?
- How do you currently handle seasonal demand spikes?
- What percentage of inventory typically becomes obsolete/unsellable?
- How do you manage inventory across multiple locations?

**Shipping & Fulfillment**
- How many shipping carriers do you use?
- What's your process for shipping exceptions/lost packages?
- Do you offer international shipping? To which countries?
- How do you handle customs documentation and duties?

**Returns & Customer Service**
- What's your current return rate by product category?
- What's your return window and restocking fee policy?
- How are refunds processed (original payment vs store credit)?
- Who pays for return shipping on defective vs. changed-mind returns?

**Regulatory & Compliance**
- What product certifications or safety standards apply?
- How do you track and manage product recalls?
- What labeling requirements apply to your products?
- Are there any age restrictions or special handling requirements?

**Insurance & Liability**
- What product liability insurance do you carry?
- How do you handle damage claims during shipping?
- What's your process for defective product claims?
- Are there any warranties offered beyond manufacturer warranties?

### **Follow-up Pathways**

**If discussing inventory management:**
- "How do you determine reorder points and quantities?"
- "What's your process for inventory counts/audits?"
- "How do you handle inventory discrepancies?"

**If discussing shipping:**
- "How do you choose carrier for each shipment?"
- "What real-time tracking information do customers expect?"
- "How do you handle address corrections?"

### **Red Flags & Required Probing**
- "We don't track inventory that closely." → Probe stockout/overstock costs
- "Returns are rare." → Probe actual return data and policy enforcement
- "We ship everywhere." → Probe international restrictions and costs
- "No special certifications needed." → Probe product category regulations

## **4. PAYMENT PROCESSING CONTEXT**

**Trigger:** Project involves monetary transactions, subscriptions, or marketplaces.

### **Primary Discovery Questions**

**Transaction Patterns**
- What's your current average transaction value?
- What's your peak transactions per minute/hour?
- What percentage of transactions fail initially?
- How do transaction patterns vary by season/day/time?

**International Considerations**
- In which currencies do you need to accept payment?
- Which payment methods are expected in each region?
- How do you handle currency conversion and exchange rates?
- What tax/VAT/GST calculations are required by region?

**Payment Models**
- What percentage of revenue comes from recurring vs. one-time?
- How do you handle failed recurring payments (dunning process)?
- Do you offer installment/payment plan options?
- How are prorated charges handled for mid-cycle changes?

**Refunds & Disputes**
- What's your current chargeback rate?
- What's your refund request process and approval workflow?
- How long do you have to respond to chargebacks?
- What evidence do you need to dispute chargebacks?

**Compliance & Security**
- What PCI compliance level do you need to maintain?
- How are payment credentials stored and handled?
- What authentication is required for high-value transactions?
- Do you need 3D Secure or similar authentication?

**Marketplace & Split Payments**
- How many parties receive funds from each transaction?
- When are funds disbursed to recipients (immediate, delayed, batched)?
- Who handles refunds/chargebacks in split payment scenarios?
- What reporting do recipients need on their transactions?

### **Follow-up Pathways**

**If discussing recurring payments:**
- "How many billing cycles before a customer becomes profitable?"
- "What's your process for upcoming renewal notifications?"
- "How do you handle voluntary vs. involuntary churn?"

**If discussing marketplaces:**
- "What's the minimum balance for payout to recipients?"
- "How are fees calculated and deducted?"
- "What happens if a recipient's account can't receive funds?"

### **Red Flags & Required Probing**
- "We'll worry about PCI compliance later." → Probe transaction volume and risk
- "All payments go through one processor." → Probe redundancy needs
- "We don't get chargebacks." → Probe dispute resolution process
- "Currency conversion happens automatically." → Probe exchange rate margins

## **5. USER-FACING APPLICATIONS CONTEXT**

**Trigger:** Project involves public users, customer portals, or B2C applications.

### **Primary Discovery Questions**

**Authentication & Authorization**
- What authentication methods do you need (password, OAuth, MFA)?
- How many distinct user roles/permission levels are needed?
- How are forgotten passwords/reset requests handled?
- What session timeout and security policies apply?

**Onboarding & Activation**
- What's your target time-to-first-value for new users?
- What minimum data is required for account creation?
- How do you guide users through initial setup?
- What metrics define an "activated" user?

**Support & Help**
- What support channels do you offer (chat, email, phone)?
- What's your target response time for each channel?
- How is support integrated within the application?
- What self-help resources are most needed?

**Feedback & Engagement**
- How do you currently collect user feedback?
- What triggers in-app surveys or feedback requests?
- How do you measure user satisfaction (NPS, CSAT)?
- What re-engagement strategies work for inactive users?

**Retention & Metrics**
- How do you define "active user" for your application?
- What's your current retention rate at 7, 30, 90 days?
- What features correlate with long-term retention?
- What lifecycle emails or notifications drive engagement?

### **Follow-up Pathways**

**If discussing user roles:**
- "How often do permissions need to change for existing users?"
- "Who approves permission changes and how?"
- "How are permissions audited for compliance?"

**If discussing onboarding:**
- "What percentage of users complete onboarding currently?"
- "Where do users drop off during onboarding?"
- "How do you re-engage users who abandon onboarding?"

### **Red Flags & Required Probing**
- "Users figure it out themselves." → Probe support volume and user frustration
- "Everyone gets full access." → Probe data sensitivity and compliance needs
- "We don't track user behavior." → Probe retention challenges
- "Onboarding is optional." → Probe activation and success rates

## **6. INTERNAL TOOLS CONTEXT**

**Trigger:** Project involves employee tools, admin systems, or workflow automation.

### **Primary Discovery Questions**

**Stakeholder Buy-in & Change Management**
- Who are the primary sponsors and detractors of this project?
- How will success be communicated to affected teams?
- What's the change management plan for rollout?
- How are reluctant users being addressed?

**Training & Adoption**
- What training approach works best for your organization?
- How will training be delivered and tracked?
- What ongoing support will power users provide?
- How will adoption be measured and encouraged?

**Administration & Maintenance**
- Who will handle day-to-day system administration?
- What technical skills do admins currently have?
- What backup and recovery procedures are needed?
- How are system updates and patches handled?

**Workflow Integration**
- Which existing systems must this tool integrate with?
- What manual processes will this tool replace?
- How will data flow between old and new systems?
- What's the cutover/transition plan?

**ROI & Success Metrics**
- What quantitative benefits justify this investment?
- How will productivity improvements be measured?
- What's the payback period expectation?
- What qualitative improvements are expected?

### **Follow-up Pathways**

**If discussing legacy system replacement:**
- "What data needs to be migrated from the old system?"
- "How will you run parallel systems during transition?"
- "What functionality exists in old system that's not in requirements?"

**If discussing user adoption:**
- "What incentives exist for users to switch to new system?"
- "How will you handle users who resist the change?"
- "What's the plan for users who can't use the new system?"

### **Red Flags & Required Probing**
- "IT will handle everything." → Probe business unit ownership
- "Training isn't in the budget." → Probe adoption risk
- "We'll figure out integration later." → Probe current workflow dependencies
- "Any improvement is good enough." → Probe success criteria and ROI expectations

## **7. API/INTEGRATION PROJECTS CONTEXT**

**Trigger:** Project involves external APIs, system integration, or developer platforms.

### **Primary Discovery Questions**

**Rate Limiting & Quotas**
- What rate limits are appropriate for different consumer tiers?
- How are quotas measured (per minute, hour, day, month)?
- How should consumers track their usage against limits?
- What happens when limits are exceeded?

**Error Handling & Retry Logic**
- What HTTP status codes will your API use?
- How detailed should error responses be?
- What retry policies should consumers implement?
- How are transient vs. permanent errors distinguished?

**Webhook Reliability**
- What guarantees does your webhook delivery provide?
- How are failed webhook deliveries retried?
- What's the maximum retry period for webhooks?
- How can consumers verify webhook authenticity?

**API Versioning**
- What versioning strategy will you use (URL, header, etc.)?
- How long will old versions be supported?
- How are breaking changes communicated to consumers?
- What's the deprecation timeline for old versions?

**Documentation & Developer Experience**
- What API documentation format works for your consumers?
- Will you provide SDKs/libraries for common languages?
- How will you handle developer support requests?
- What testing environment/sandbox will you provide?

### **Follow-up Pathways**

**If discussing authentication:**
- "What authentication methods will you support (API keys, OAuth, etc.)?"
- "How are credentials issued, revoked, and rotated?"
- "What's the process for compromised credentials?"

**If discussing data formats:**
- "What request/response formats will you support (JSON, XML, etc.)?"
- "How will you handle versioning of data schemas?"
- "What validation errors will you return for malformed requests?"

### **Red Flags & Required Probing**
- "We don't need rate limiting." → Probe expected usage patterns and abuse prevention
- "All errors return 500 status." → Probe debugging and integration complexity
- "Versioning isn't necessary." → Probe change management and consumer impact
- "Documentation will come later." → Probe adoption barriers and support costs

## **DISCOVERY FRAMEWORK GUIDANCE**

### **When to Use Which Question Bank**

**Project Initiation Phase:** Use all relevant context question banks to identify which areas need exploration.

**Requirements Gathering Sessions:** Focus on the primary context plus 1-2 adjacent contexts (e.g., Physical Goods + Payment Processing).

**Risk Assessment Workshops:** Use red flag questions across all contexts to identify potential project risks.

**Stakeholder Interviews:** Tailor questions based on stakeholder domain (business, technical, operational).

### **Question Sequencing Strategy**

1. **Open with context-establishing questions:** "What type of business/problem is this?"
2. **Move to current state questions:** "How do you handle this currently?"
3. **Probe pain points:** "What's most frustrating about the current approach?"
4. **Explore constraints:** "What limitations or requirements must we respect?"
5. **Identify success criteria:** "What would make this project successful?"
6. **Uncover hidden requirements:** "What haven't we discussed that could derail this?"

### **Adaptation for Project Size**

**Small Projects (< 3 months):** Focus on red flag questions and primary pain points.

**Medium Projects (3-9 months):** Use full context question banks with emphasis on integration points.

**Large Projects (> 9 months):** Conduct formal discovery workshops for each relevant context.

### **Documentation & Tracking**

For each project context, document:
- Which questions revealed new information
- Which assumptions were invalidated
- Which red flags were identified and mitigated
- Which stakeholders provided which perspectives

This creates institutional knowledge and improves future discovery processes.

## **CONTEXT TRIGGER MATRIX**

| Project Characteristic | Primary Context | Secondary Contexts to Include |
|----------------------|----------------|------------------------------|
| "Clients pay for our time" | Service Business | Payment Processing, User-Facing Apps |
| "We sell products online" | Physical Goods | Payment Processing, Forms & Data Collection |
| "Users submit applications" | Forms & Data Collection | User-Facing Apps, Payment Processing |
| "Monthly subscriptions" | Payment Processing | User-Facing Apps, Internal Tools |
| "Employee workflow system" | Internal Tools | API/Integration, Forms & Data Collection |
| "Partner integration" | API/Integration | Payment Processing, Internal Tools |
| "Customer portal" | User-Facing Apps | Authentication requirements from Service Business or Physical Goods |

*This matrix helps quickly identify which question banks to deploy based on initial project descriptions.*

**Usage Note:** This question library is designed to be searchable by context trigger. When beginning discovery on a new project, identify the primary context from the trigger statements, then expand to secondary contexts based on the matrix above. Always follow red flag responses with the probing questions provided. Document which questions yield new insights to refine your discovery process over time.
