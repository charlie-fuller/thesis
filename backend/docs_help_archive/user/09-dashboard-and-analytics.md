# Dashboard and Analytics

Understand your productivity and impact with the My Impact dashboard.

## Accessing Your Dashboard

Click **My Impact** in the user menu (your name/avatar in the top navigation) to view your personal analytics dashboard.

The dashboard shows metrics about your Thesis usage and L&D productivity over time.

## Dashboard Overview

Your dashboard displays three metric cards, each tracking a different aspect of your work with Thesis:

1. **Activity Summary** - Tracks your overall engagement with Thesis
2. **Design Velocity** - Measures your content development pace
3. **Systems Thinking** - Evaluates the quality of your instructional design approach

Use the time period selector at the top to view metrics for:
- **Week** - Last 7 days of activity
- **Month** - Last 30 days of activity (default)
- **All Time** - Complete history

## Metric Cards Explained

### Activity Summary

**What it measures:** Your overall engagement and usage of Thesis.

**Metrics shown:**
- **Conversations** - Total conversations started
- **Messages** - Total messages sent
- **Documents** - Documents uploaded
- **Images** - Images generated

**What to look for:**
- Increasing activity indicates growing adoption and comfort with the platform
- Balanced metrics across conversations and messages suggests healthy engagement
- Document uploads show you're leveraging context for better responses
- Image generation reflects visual content creation for your training materials

### Design Velocity

**What it measures:** How consistently you're developing L&D programs and content.

**Indicators:**
- **Project Days** - Number of unique days with learning design activity
- Shows as a metric like "8 project days this month"
- Includes trend comparison to previous period (e.g., "+2 vs. last month")

**What to look for:**
- Higher project days = more consistent work pattern
- Increasing velocity suggests improved productivity
- Consistent velocity indicates stable work rhythm
- Compare week vs. month to identify patterns

**Understanding Project Days:**
A "project day" is any day where you engaged in learning design activities with Thesis. This metric helps you:
- Track your consistency in using Thesis for L&D work
- Identify productive periods vs. slow periods
- Set goals for regular engagement

### Systems Thinking

**What it measures:** The quality and comprehensiveness of your instructional design approach.

**Overall Score:** 0-100 score measuring design thinking quality across six dimensions.

**The Six Dimensions:**

| Dimension | What It Evaluates | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Learner Focus** | Understanding audience needs, context, and prerequisites | Ensures training is relevant and accessible |
| **Outcome Orientation** | Clear objectives, success criteria, and measurable results | Drives ROI and business impact |
| **Transfer & Application** | Real-world relevance and on-the-job application | Maximizes learning retention and behavior change |
| **Engagement Design** | Active learning, interaction, and learner participation | Increases motivation and completion rates |
| **Retention & Reinforcement** | Memory techniques, spacing, repetition, and follow-up | Ensures long-term retention and skill development |
| **Feedback & Assessment** | Measuring progress, providing feedback, and checking understanding | Validates learning and identifies gaps |

**Each dimension shows:**
- Percentage score (0-100%)
- Number of conversations where this dimension was addressed

**What to look for:**
- **High overall score (80+)** - Demonstrates comprehensive instructional design thinking
- **Balanced dimensions** - Shows well-rounded approach to L&D design
- **Low scores in specific dimensions** - Indicates areas for professional development
- **Increasing scores over time** - Reflects growing proficiency in instructional design

**Using Systems Thinking Scores:**
- Review scores weekly to identify which design dimensions you naturally emphasize
- Focus on lower-scoring dimensions in upcoming projects to develop balanced skills
- High scores validate your thoroughness in addressing learner needs
- Track improvement over time as you develop expertise

## Understanding Your Metrics

### Time Periods

The time period selector at the top of the dashboard controls all three cards:

- **Week** - Shows data for the last 7 days
- **Month** - Shows data for the last 30 days (default)
- **All Time** - Shows your complete history with Thesis

Choose different periods to:
- Spot recent trends (Week)
- Identify monthly patterns (Month)
- See overall growth (All Time)

### Interpreting Trends

**Design Velocity Trends:**
The Design Velocity card shows comparison to the previous period:
- **"+5 vs. last month"** - You had 5 more project days this month than last month (increasing productivity)
- **"-2 vs. last month"** - You had 2 fewer project days this month (decreasing activity)
- **"No change"** - Same number of project days as previous period (stable pattern)

Use trends to:
- Identify if you're becoming more consistent in your work
- Spot periods of high productivity
- Recognize when you might need to increase engagement

## Using Dashboard Insights

### Identify Patterns

Review your dashboard weekly to spot trends:

- **High activity + high project days + high systems thinking** = Optimal performance, keep it up
- **Low activity but high systems thinking** = Quality over quantity, good depth
- **High activity but low systems thinking scores** = May need to slow down and think more strategically
- **Consistent metrics across all cards** = Stable, productive state

### Set Personal Goals

Use metrics as benchmarks:

- "Increase project days from 8 to 12 this month"
- "Improve Systems Thinking score from 65 to 75"
- "Upload at least 5 documents each month to improve context"
- "Maintain at least 10 project days per month"

### Track Improvement

Check your dashboard regularly after implementing changes:

- **Focused on learner needs more?** Check Learner Focus dimension score
- **Being more intentional about objectives?** Check Outcome Orientation score
- **Using Thesis more consistently?** Check Design Velocity project days
- **Uploading more context documents?** Check Activity Summary document count

### Systems Thinking Growth

Your Systems Thinking score is one of the most valuable metrics for professional development:

**If you have low scores in specific dimensions:**
- **Learner Focus** - Start conversations by describing your audience in detail
- **Outcome Orientation** - Always specify clear learning objectives and success criteria
- **Transfer & Application** - Ask how learners will apply skills on the job
- **Engagement Design** - Request interactive activities and active learning strategies
- **Retention & Reinforcement** - Include spacing, repetition, and follow-up in your designs
- **Feedback & Assessment** - Always design assessments and feedback mechanisms

**Balanced high scores (70+ across all dimensions) indicate:**
- Comprehensive instructional design thinking
- Systematic approach to L&D work
- Readiness to tackle complex learning challenges
- Strong foundation in evidence-based design practices

## Refreshing Dashboard Data

Dashboard data updates automatically based on your activity. If you want to see the latest:

1. Refresh the page
2. Wait for data to load (typically 1-2 seconds)

There's no manual refresh needed - the dashboard reflects your current state.

## Privacy and Data

- Dashboard metrics are **personal to you**
- Administrators see **aggregate analytics**, not your individual metrics
- Your conversation content is **not shared** in analytics
- Metrics are calculated from **usage patterns**, not content analysis
- Systems Thinking scores are based on instructional design best practices, not judgment of your work

## Troubleshooting

### No Data Showing

- **New user?** Metrics need some usage history to display
- **Recent activity?** Dashboard requires activity to calculate metrics
- **Try refreshing** the page if data seems stale
- **Check time period** - Switch to "All Time" to see if you have historical data

### Metrics Seem Wrong

- Check the **time period** selected (Week/Month/All Time)
- Remember metrics are **aggregated**, not per-conversation
- Some metrics update **shortly after activity**, others may take a few minutes
- Systems Thinking scores reflect the **depth of instructional design thinking** in your conversations

### Dashboard Loading Slowly

- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page
- Contact support if issues persist

### Understanding Systems Thinking Scores

**"Why is my score low?"**
- Low scores indicate opportunities to incorporate more instructional design best practices
- You might be using Thesis for quick tasks rather than comprehensive design work
- Consider addressing more of the six dimensions in your conversations

**"My score seems high but I'm new to L&D"**
- High scores reflect asking the right questions and considering multiple design factors
- This is a positive indicator - you're approaching L&D work systematically
- Continue building on this strong foundation

**"One dimension is always low"**
- This highlights an area for professional development
- Review resources on that specific aspect of instructional design
- Intentionally address that dimension in your next few projects
