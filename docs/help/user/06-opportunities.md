# Opportunities

The AI opportunity pipeline. Where you track, score, and prioritize potential AI initiatives.

Because not every AI idea is worth pursuing, and you need a way to figure out which ones are.

---

## The Pipeline

Go to **Opportunities** in the navigation.

You'll see opportunities organized by tier:
- **Tier 1** - High priority (score 16-20)
- **Tier 2** - Medium-high (score 12-15)
- **Tier 3** - Medium-low (score 8-11)
- **Tier 4** - Low priority (score 4-7)

Tiers expand and collapse. High-tier opportunities are what you should focus on.

---

## The Scoring System

Each opportunity is scored on four dimensions (1-5 each):

**ROI Potential**
How much value could this deliver?
- 5: Transformative impact, significant revenue/savings
- 3: Moderate impact, solid business case
- 1: Minimal financial benefit

**Implementation Effort**
How hard is this to do? (Inverted - high score = easier)
- 5: Quick win, low complexity
- 3: Moderate effort, some dependencies
- 1: Major initiative, many moving parts

**Strategic Alignment**
How well does this fit company priorities?
- 5: Direct alignment with stated strategy
- 3: Indirect connection to priorities
- 1: Nice to have, not strategic

**Stakeholder Readiness**
Are the people ready for this?
- 5: Champions exist, low resistance expected
- 3: Mixed support, some change management needed
- 1: Significant resistance, political challenges

Total score determines the tier. It's a simple framework, but it forces you to think through multiple dimensions.

## Creating Opportunities

Click **+ New Opportunity**.

**Required:**
- Name
- Description
- All four scores

**Optional:**
- Department
- Owner (links to a stakeholder)
- Status

**The form walks you through the scoring.** Each dimension has guidelines for what each score means.

---

## Status Tracking

Opportunities move through a lifecycle:

1. **Identified** - You've spotted the opportunity
2. **Validating** - Actively assessing feasibility
3. **Approved** - Green-lit to proceed
4. **Implementing** - Work in progress
5. **Completed** - Done and delivered

Also:
- **Blocked** - Something's in the way

Status changes are tracked. You can see the history.

---

## Filtering

At the top of the page:
- **By Department** - Focus on a specific area
- **By Status** - See what's in progress vs. blocked

---

## Linking to Stakeholders

Set an **Owner** to connect an opportunity to a stakeholder.

This lets you:
- See which stakeholders own which opportunities
- Track who's driving what
- Connect your opportunity pipeline to your relationship map

## How Operator Uses This

The Operator agent has access to your opportunity pipeline.

When you ask Operator about priorities, triage, or what to work on next, it pulls from this data:
- Current opportunities by tier
- Blocked items
- Status distribution

> "What should I focus on this week?"

Operator can reference your actual pipeline to answer.

## The Tier Summary

At the top of the Opportunities page, you'll see summary cards:
- Count of opportunities per tier
- Quick visibility into your portfolio

If everything is Tier 4, you might need better opportunities. If everything is Tier 1, you might need to be more honest about scoring.

---

## Editing Opportunities

Click any opportunity to open its detail view.

You can:
- Update scores (tier recalculates automatically)
- Change status
- Update owner
- Add notes

---

## Best Practices

**Score honestly.** It's tempting to inflate scores to make something look better. Resist.

**Review tiers regularly.** As you learn more, scores change. An opportunity that seemed like a quick win might turn out to be harder.

**Link to stakeholders.** Every opportunity should have an owner.

**Don't hoard.** Remove opportunities that aren't going anywhere. A clean pipeline is easier to work with.

**Use status actively.** Move things through the lifecycle. Blocked items need attention or removal.

---

## What Opportunities Are For

Track *potential AI initiatives* - things you might do, are evaluating, or are implementing.

**Use Opportunities for:**
- AI use cases you're considering
- Automation possibilities
- GenAI applications being evaluated

**Don't use Opportunities for:**
- Day-to-day tasks (use Tasks)
- Documents and research (use Knowledge Base)
- Stakeholder tracking (use Intelligence)
