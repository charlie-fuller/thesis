# Stakeholder Intelligence

The humans matter most. This is where you track them.

---

## The Intelligence Page

Go to **Intelligence** in the navigation.

You'll see:
- **Engagement Trends** - Visualization of stakeholder engagement over time
- **Stakeholder List** - Everyone you're tracking
- **Key Metrics** - Summary statistics

---

## What Gets Tracked

For each stakeholder:

**Basic Info:**
- Name, title, department
- Reports to (org context)
- Team size
- Communication style preference

**Relationship Data:**
- Priority level (Tier 1/2/3)
- Relationship status (new, warming, established, champion, cooling)
- Last contact date

**AI Context:**
- AI priorities (what they care about)
- Pain points (what's frustrating them)
- Win conditions (what success looks like to them)
- Open questions (things to address next)

**Engagement Level:**
- Champion, Supporter, Neutral, Skeptic, or Blocker
- Calculated from interaction patterns
- History tracked for trends

---

## Adding Stakeholders

You can add stakeholders:

1. **Manually** - Click Add Stakeholder, fill in details
2. **From Transcripts** - Oracle extracts them from meeting transcripts
3. **Auto-linked** - System matches names in transcripts to existing stakeholders

The transcript-to-stakeholder flow is powerful. Upload a meeting, Oracle identifies participants and links them.

---

## Engagement Levels

The system calculates engagement levels automatically:

**Champion** (best):
- 5+ interactions AND (1+ commitment OR 3+ support signals)

**Supporter:**
- 3+ interactions AND >50% positive sentiment

**Neutral:**
- Default state

**Skeptic/Blocker:**
- Requires unresolved objections

**Sticky levels:** You don't get demoted to a lower level without explicit negative signals. One bad meeting doesn't turn a Champion into a Skeptic.

---

## Engagement Trends

The visualization shows how engagement levels shift over time.

**Why this matters:** Patterns tell you things:
- A Champion going quiet might signal something
- A Skeptic moving to Supporter shows your approach is working
- Overall trend up or down across stakeholders

The trend chart uses a stacked area view. You can see the composition of your stakeholder map at any point.

## Meeting Prep Pages

Click a stakeholder to see their **Meeting Prep** page.

This is the view to pull up before you meet with them:

**Status Summary:**
- Red/yellow/green indicators across key areas

**Linked Opportunities:**
- AI opportunities they own or care about

**Open Questions:**
- Things to address in your next conversation

**AI-Recommended Approach:**
- Suggestions based on their profile

**Days Since Last Contact:**
- Accountability reminder

It's like having briefing notes that actually tell you something useful.

## Extracting Stakeholder Insights

When Oracle analyzes a meeting transcript, it extracts:
- Who was in the room
- What they said (quotes as evidence)
- Their sentiment
- Concerns raised
- Commitments made

This flows into stakeholder records. Over time, you build a picture of each person's perspective.

---

## The Stakeholder Graph

Behind the scenes, stakeholders connect to other data:
- **Opportunities** they own
- **Concerns** they've raised
- **Meetings** they've attended
- **Tasks** assigned to them

This graph context gets injected into agent responses. When you ask about a stakeholder, agents know the full picture.

---

## Best Practices

**Keep it current.** Update after every significant interaction.

**Use the open questions field.** It's your prep for the next meeting.

**Note communication style.** Some people want data, some want story, some want brevity. Tracking this helps.

**Review engagement trends.** Patterns over time tell you more than point-in-time status.

**Upload transcripts regularly.** The more data Oracle has, the better the analysis.

**Link opportunities.** Every opportunity should have a stakeholder owner.

---

## What Stakeholder Intelligence Is For

Track *relationships and people dynamics* in your AI initiatives.

**Use Intelligence for:**
- Stakeholder tracking
- Engagement monitoring
- Meeting prep
- Relationship health

**Don't use Intelligence for:**
- Tasks (use Tasks page)
- Opportunities (use Opportunities page)
- Documents (use Knowledge Base)

---

## Connecting the Dots

Stakeholder Intelligence connects to everything else:

- **Meeting Rooms** → Oracle extracts stakeholder insights
- **Opportunities** → Stakeholders own opportunities
- **Tasks** → Tasks assigned to stakeholders
- **Knowledge Base** → Stakeholder-relevant documents

The goal is a unified view of the humans in your AI initiatives.
