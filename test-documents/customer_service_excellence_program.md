# Customer Service Excellence Program
## Learning & Development Initiative

### Executive Summary
This L&D program addresses declining customer satisfaction scores (CSAT dropped from 4.2 to 3.6 over 6 months) by upskilling our customer service team in de-escalation techniques, empathetic communication, and problem-solving frameworks.

**Target Audience:** 120 customer service representatives across 3 call centers
**Duration:** 8-week blended learning program
**Business Goal:** Increase CSAT from 3.6 to 4.5+ within 90 days post-training

---

## Business Context & Performance Gap

### Current State (DDLD Analysis)
**Data:**
- CSAT score: 3.6/5.0 (down from 4.2)
- Average call resolution time: 12 minutes (up from 8 minutes)
- First-call resolution rate: 62% (down from 78%)
- Customer escalation rate: 24% (up from 12%)

**Desired State:**
- CSAT score: 4.5+/5.0
- Average call resolution time: 8 minutes or less
- First-call resolution rate: 85%+
- Customer escalation rate: <10%

**Learning Gap:**
Customer service representatives lack structured frameworks for:
1. De-escalating emotionally charged customer interactions
2. Active listening and empathetic response techniques
3. Root cause analysis for customer issues
4. Proactive problem-solving vs. reactive responses

**Difference/Impact:**
Current reactive approach creates longer call times, frustrated customers, and increased escalations. Projected revenue impact: $2.4M annual loss from customer churn.

---

## Target Behavior Changes

Upon completion, customer service representatives will:

1. **Use the LEAP Framework** for every escalated call:
   - **L**isten actively without interrupting
   - **E**mpathize with the customer's emotional state
   - **A**cknowledge the specific issue and impact
   - **P**ropose solutions with customer involvement

2. **Apply Root Cause Analysis** using "5 Whys" technique within first 3 minutes of call

3. **Document interactions** using structured templates that capture:
   - Customer emotional state
   - Root cause identified
   - Solution proposed and accepted
   - Follow-up actions committed

4. **Proactively suggest** solutions before customers ask, based on pattern recognition from previous similar cases

---

## Learning Objectives

### Knowledge Objectives
Learners will be able to:
- Explain the psychological principles behind de-escalation techniques
- Identify the five stages of customer emotional escalation
- Describe the components of the LEAP framework
- List common customer pain points and their root causes

### Skill Objectives
Learners will be able to:
- Demonstrate active listening techniques in live call scenarios
- Apply the LEAP framework to de-escalate angry customers
- Conduct root cause analysis using the "5 Whys" method
- Document customer interactions using structured templates

### Performance Objectives
On the job, learners will:
- Achieve 85%+ first-call resolution rate within 30 days
- Reduce average call time to 8 minutes or less
- Maintain CSAT scores of 4.5+ on 90% of interactions
- Decrease escalation rate to <10% within 60 days

## Program Structure

### Week 1-2: Foundation Phase
**Delivery:** Asynchronous eLearning + Virtual Classroom
- Module 1: Customer Psychology & Emotional Intelligence (2 hours)
- Module 2: Active Listening & Empathetic Communication (3 hours)
- Virtual Practice Session 1: LEAP Framework Role-Plays (90 minutes)

### Week 3-4: Application Phase
**Delivery:** On-the-Job Practice + Coaching
- Daily 15-minute coaching huddles with team leads
- Shadow calls: Observe 5 high-performing reps using LEAP framework
- Guided practice: 10 supervised calls with immediate feedback

### Week 5-6: Mastery Phase
**Delivery:** Independent Practice + Peer Learning
- Independent application of LEAP framework on all calls
- Weekly peer learning circles (60 minutes): Share challenging cases
- Self-assessment: Record and review 3 calls per week

### Week 7-8: Sustainment Phase
**Delivery:** Performance Support + Reinforcement
- Job aids embedded in CRM system (LEAP quick reference, 5 Whys template)
- Bi-weekly refresher microlearning (5-minute videos)
- Team celebration: Recognition for top performers

---

## Assessment Strategy

### Formative Assessments (During Learning)
- Knowledge checks after each eLearning module
- Role-play observations with rubric scoring
- Peer feedback on recorded practice calls

### Summative Assessments (End of Program)
- Simulated customer call assessment (pass/fail based on LEAP framework application)
- Written case study: Analyze complex customer scenario and propose solution
- Manager observation: 3 live calls scored against performance rubric

### Level 3 Evaluation (Behavior Transfer)
- 30-day post-training audit: Random sampling of 20 calls per learner
- Manager observations: Weekly 1:1 coaching conversations
- Self-reported survey: Confidence in applying LEAP framework

### Level 4 Evaluation (Business Impact)
- CSAT scores tracked weekly (target: 4.5+)
- First-call resolution rate (target: 85%+)
- Average call time (target: ≤8 minutes)
- Customer escalation rate (target: <10%)

## Success Metrics & ROI

### Leading Indicators (30 days post-training)
- 90% of learners pass simulated call assessment
- 85% of learners achieve first-call resolution rate of 75%+

- First-call resolution rate reaches 85%+
- Customer escalation rate drops to <10%

## Risk Mitigation

**Risk:** Learners revert to old habits under time pressure
**Mitigation:** Embed LEAP framework prompts in CRM system; weekly manager check-ins

**Risk:** Inconsistent coaching quality across 3 call centers
**Mitigation:** Train-the-trainer program for team leads; standardized coaching rubrics

**Risk:** Business leaders don't see immediate results and cancel program
**Mitigation:** Weekly leading indicator dashboard shared with stakeholders; celebrate small wins
