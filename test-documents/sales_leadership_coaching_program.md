# Sales Leadership Coaching Program
## Developing High-Impact Sales Managers

### Program Overview
Transform first-line sales managers from individual contributors promoted to leadership into effective coaches who develop high-performing sales teams.

**Target Audience:** 45 sales managers (promoted from top-performing sales reps in past 18 months)
**Duration:** 12-week program (blended: virtual + in-person + on-the-job practice)
**Business Driver:** Sales team quota attainment declined from 89% to 67% after manager promotions

---

## Business Problem (DDLD Framework)

### Data
- **Team quota attainment:** 67% (down from 89% pre-promotion)
- **Sales rep turnover:** 34% annually (up from 18%)
- **Pipeline velocity:** 47 days (up from 32 days)
- **Manager time allocation:** 70% on personal deals, 15% on coaching, 15% on admin
- **Revenue impact:** $8.2M missed quota (annualized)

### Desired State
- **Team quota attainment:** 85%+ within 6 months
- **Sales rep turnover:** <20% annually
- **Pipeline velocity:** 35 days or less
- **Manager time allocation:** 20% on personal deals, 60% on coaching, 20% on strategic planning
- **Revenue recovery:** $6M+ of missed quota

### Learning Gap
Sales managers lack:
1. Structured coaching frameworks (vs. just telling reps what to do)
2. Skills to diagnose rep performance gaps accurately
3. Ability to deliver developmental feedback without demotivating
4. Pipeline inspection techniques to forecast accurately
5. Time management strategies to balance player-coach role

### Difference/Impact
Managers continue to "do" instead of "develop," creating dependency and limiting team scalability. High performers leave due to lack of development. Revenue suffers.

---

## Critical Behavior Changes

Post-program, sales managers will:

1. **Conduct weekly 1:1 coaching conversations** using GROW model:
   - **G**oal: What does the rep want to achieve this week?
   - **R**eality: What's actually happening in their pipeline?
   - **O**ptions: What could they try? (Manager guides, doesn't tell)
   - **W**ay Forward: What will they commit to?

2. **Inspect pipeline health** in team meetings using:
   - Stage conversion rates (Lead → Opp → Proposal → Close)
   - Deal velocity by stage
   - Win/loss patterns by rep

3. **Deliver feedback** using SBI-I model:
   - **S**ituation: When/where did this happen?
   - **B**ehavior: What specifically did I observe?
   - **I**mpact: What was the effect on results/team?
   - **I**nvite: What do you think? How can I support?

4. **Delegate personal deals** to team members as development opportunities (reduce personal deal load to 20% of time)

## Learning Outcomes

### Knowledge
- Explain the difference between coaching, mentoring, and managing
- Describe the GROW coaching model and when to use it
- Identify the 5 stages of sales skill development (Novice → Expert)
- List common pipeline health metrics and their diagnostic value

### Skills
- Conduct effective 1:1 coaching conversations using GROW model
- Deliver developmental feedback using SBI-I framework
- Analyze pipeline data to identify team performance trends
- Create individual development plans for each team member

### On-the-Job Performance
- 85% of managers conduct weekly 1:1s with all direct reports
- Team quota attainment increases to 80%+ within 90 days
- Sales rep engagement scores increase by 20%+ (measured via pulse surveys)
- Pipeline forecast accuracy improves to ±10%

## Program Design

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-3)
**Delivery:** Virtual Instructor-Led Training (VILT)

**Week 1: From Seller to Coach**
- Mindset shift: Your job is to develop, not do
- Self-assessment: Current time allocation vs. ideal
- Introduction to GROW coaching model
- Practice: Role-play coaching conversations (3 scenarios)

**Week 2: Diagnostic Coaching**
- Sales skill development stages (Novice → Expert)
- Identifying performance gaps: Skill vs. Will vs. Obstacles
- Practice: Analyze 5 real rep scenarios and diagnose root cause

**Week 3: Feedback That Develops**
- SBI-I feedback model
- Balancing positive reinforcement and constructive feedback
- Practice: Deliver feedback on recorded sales calls (peer review)

**Homework:** Each manager conducts 3 GROW coaching conversations with their reps and submits recordings for feedback

---

### Phase 2: Application (Weeks 4-8)
**Delivery:** On-the-Job Practice + Weekly Virtual Coaching Labs

**Weekly Coaching Labs (60 minutes):**
- Share challenging coaching situations from the week
- Peer feedback and problem-solving
- Expert facilitator guidance (external sales leadership coach)

**On-the-Job Application:**
- Conduct weekly 1:1s using GROW model (minimum 4 per week)
- Shadow 2 peer managers' team meetings to observe different styles
- Create individual development plans for each direct report

**Manager Support:**
- Bi-weekly 1:1s with their own manager to review progress
- Access to coaching conversation templates and job aids
- Slack channel for peer support and questions

**Milestone Assessment (Week 6):**
- Submit 3 recorded coaching conversations for expert review
- Peer feedback session: Each manager shares one success and one challenge

---

### Phase 3: Mastery (Weeks 9-12)
**Delivery:** In-Person Workshop + Ongoing Practice

**Week 9-10: In-Person Workshop (2 days)**
**Day 1: Pipeline Leadership**
- Morning: Pipeline health metrics and diagnostics
  - Stage conversion analysis
  - Deal velocity by rep and stage
  - Win/loss pattern identification
- Afternoon: Practice with real team pipeline data
  - Each manager presents their team's pipeline health
  - Group problem-solving: What's working? What needs attention?

**Day 2: Advanced Coaching Scenarios**
- Morning: Coaching difficult conversations
  - Underperformers: When to coach vs. manage out
  - High performers: Preventing burnout and retention
  - Team conflicts: Mediating without taking sides
- Afternoon: Role-play simulations (actors play challenging reps)
  - Scenario 1: High performer threatening to leave
  - Scenario 2: Veteran rep resistant to coaching
  - Scenario 3: New rep struggling with confidence

**Week 11-12: Integration & Reinforcement**
- Continue weekly 1:1s and coaching labs
- Managers present team improvement stories (5-minute videos)
- Create personal development plan for their own growth as a leader

## Assessment & Evaluation

### Formative (During Program)
- Weekly self-reflection journals: What went well? What would I do differently?
- Peer observation: Each manager observes 2 peers' coaching conversations
- Expert review: 3 recorded coaching conversations scored against rubric

### Summative (End of Program)
- **Live coaching assessment:** Conduct 30-minute coaching conversation with actor (playing struggling rep) - scored by 2 evaluators using rubric
- **Pipeline analysis case study:** Given team pipeline data, diagnose health and propose action plan
- **Personal development plan:** Submit 90-day plan for their own leadership growth

### Level 3 (Behavior Transfer - 30/60/90 days)
- Manager survey: Self-reported frequency of coaching behaviors
- Rep survey: "My manager provides developmental coaching weekly" (agree/disagree)
- Manager observation: Their own leader observes 1 team meeting and 1 coaching conversation

### Level 4 (Business Impact - 90/180 days)
- Team quota attainment (target: 80%+ by day 90, 85%+ by day 180)
- Sales rep turnover rate (target: <20% annually)
- Pipeline velocity (target: 35 days or less)
- Manager time allocation (target: 60% coaching, measured via calendar audit)

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|---------------------|
| Managers don't have time for weekly 1:1s | Reduce personal deal load by 50%; provide calendar templates |
| Senior leaders don't reinforce coaching behaviors | Engage VP of Sales as executive sponsor; include coaching in manager performance reviews |
| Reps resist being coached | Communicate program to reps in advance; emphasize development benefits |
| Managers revert to "telling" instead of coaching | Monthly refresher sessions; peer accountability groups |
